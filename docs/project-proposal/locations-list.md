# Places of Interest

---

## 1. Matara Fort

**Category:** Historical Landmark  
**Distance from Matara:** 0.5 km

Built by the Dutch in 1763, Matara Fort stands at the mouth of the Nilwala River in the heart of Matara town. The fort walls and ramparts remain largely intact, offering a well-preserved example of Dutch colonial military architecture in Sri Lanka. Visitors can walk along the old ramparts, explore the surrounding streets lined with colonial-era buildings, and enjoy scenic views of the Indian Ocean.

---

## 2. Star Fort and Museum

**Category:** Historical Landmark  
**Distance from Matara:** 1 km

The Star Fort is a small but distinctive six-pointed star-shaped fortification built by the Dutch in 1765, located just outside the main Matara Fort. It now houses a regional museum featuring artefacts, antique weapons, and exhibits on the history of Matara under Dutch and British colonial rule. The fort's unique geometric design makes it one of the most photographed historical structures in southern Sri Lanka.

---

## 3. Old Dutch Trade Centre / Nupe Market

**Category:** Heritage Site  
**Distance from Matara:** 1 km

The Old Dutch Trade Centre, commonly known as Nupe Market, is one of the oldest commercial buildings in Matara, dating back to the Dutch colonial period. The structure retains much of its original architectural character and continues to serve as a local market, offering visitors a living glimpse into the town's mercantile heritage. It is an important reminder of Matara's role as a key trading post during the 17th and 18th centuries.

---

## 4. Dutch Reformed Church

**Category:** Heritage and Religious Site  
**Distance from Matara:** 0.8 km

Located within the Matara Fort precinct, the Dutch Reformed Church is one of the oldest Christian churches in Sri Lanka, constructed during the Dutch colonial era. The church features classic colonial architecture with thick whitewashed walls and a simple interior. Although no longer in regular use for worship, it is maintained as a heritage site and is open to visitors interested in the island's colonial religious history.

---

## 5. Paravi Duwa Buddhist Temple

**Category:** Religious Site  
**Distance from Matara:** 1.5 km

Paravi Duwa, meaning "Pigeon Island" in Sinhala, is a small island temple accessible by a footbridge from Matara beach. The temple is set against a striking backdrop of sea and rock, making it one of the most scenic religious sites in the south. It is an active place of worship and is particularly atmospheric at dusk, when the sounds of evening prayers carry across the water.

---

## 6. Weherahena Temple

**Category:** Religious and Cultural Site  
**Distance from Matara:** 3 km

Weherahena Temple is a major Buddhist complex located on the outskirts of Matara, famous for its underground tunnel adorned with hundreds of brightly coloured murals depicting scenes from the life of the Buddha and Buddhist teachings. The temple also features a large seated Buddha statue that dominates the site. It is one of the most visited religious sites in the Southern Province and holds significant cultural importance.

---

---

## 8. Dondra Head Lighthouse

**Category:** Natural and Historical Landmark  
**Distance from Matara:** 6 km

Located at Dondra, the southernmost point of Sri Lanka, the Dondra Head Lighthouse is the tallest lighthouse on the island, standing at approximately 49 metres. Built by the British in 1890, the lighthouse offers panoramic views of the Indian Ocean from its upper gallery on days when it is accessible. The surrounding area, including the ancient Dondra Devalaya temple complex, adds further historical and cultural significance to the site.

---

## 9. Polhena Beach

**Category:** Beach and Snorkelling  
**Distance from Matara:** 6 km

Polhena Beach is one of the most popular and family-friendly beaches in the Matara area, sheltered by a natural coral reef that creates calm, shallow waters ideal for swimming and snorkelling. The reef is home to a variety of marine life, including resident sea turtles that can regularly be spotted close to shore. Snorkelling equipment is available for hire from local dive centres, making it an accessible destination for visitors of all experience levels. The beach is best visited on weekdays to avoid local weekend crowds.

---

## 10. Mirissa Beach and Coconut Tree Hill

**Category:** Beach and Scenic Viewpoint  
**Distance from Matara:** 12 km

Mirissa is a picturesque beach town known for its crescent-shaped bay, relaxed atmosphere, and world-class whale watching from November to April. Coconut Tree Hill, a small promontory covered with leaning coconut palms overlooking the bay, is one of the most photographed viewpoints on the southern coast. The area offers a range of water activities, beachside dining, and boat tours, making it a versatile destination for both relaxation and adventure.

---

## 11. Nilwala River Safari

**Category:** Nature and Wildlife  
**Distance from Matara:** 2 km

The Nilwala River flows through Matara before emptying into the Indian Ocean, and the stretch upstream from the town offers rewarding river safari experiences by boat. Visitors can observe a variety of bird species, monitor lizards, and other wildlife along the riverbanks. The safari is typically conducted in the early morning or late afternoon for the best wildlife sightings and is suitable for nature enthusiasts and families alike.

---

## 12. Madiha Beach

**Category:** Beach  
**Distance from Matara:** 10 km

Madiha Beach is a quieter, less commercialised alternative to the more popular beaches along the southern coast. Situated between Matara and Mirissa, it is characterised by soft golden sand, clear water, and a peaceful atmosphere that appeals to visitors seeking a more secluded beach experience. The beach is relatively undeveloped, retaining a natural charm that distinguishes it from the busier tourist hotspots nearby.
